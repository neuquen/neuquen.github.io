EMPLOYMENT TRENDS

Unemployment has been an ongoing issue across the US and there are many factors which contribute to it. According to Frey and Osborne from Oxford University, "While there is ongoing disagreement about the driving forces behind the persistently high unemployment rates, a number of scholars have pointed at computer-controlled equipment as a possible explanation for recent jobless growth."

It is well established that jobs which required routine, well-defined tasks are more likely to be computerized and jobs which require problem-solving skills and higher education are less likely to be computerized. For this reason, we analysed the job market for both employed and unemployed persons since 2000 to determine if there was a correlation.
